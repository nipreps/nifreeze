# EPI susceptibility-induced (off-resonance) distortion

The geometric distortion of echo-planar images (EPI, the readout used for
diffusion and BOLD) caused by static off-resonance fields. Notation follows the
"Definition of terms" and Appendix of [[andersson-2016-integrated-eddy]].

## Origin

EPI traverses k-space slowly in the **phase-encode (PE) direction**, so its
low effective bandwidth in that direction makes it exquisitely sensitive to
off-resonance. A field offset $\psi$ (in Hz) at a voxel displaces that voxel
**along the PE direction** by an amount proportional to $\psi$ and to the
acquisition's PE bandwidth. Writing $a$ for the acquisition parameters (PE
direction and total readout time / bandwidth), the voxel displacement field is
denoted $d(\psi, a)$, and (Appendix Eq. A3) points along the PE axis:

$$ d_x = \big[\, t\, p_1\, \psi_x \quad t\, p_2\, \psi_x \quad t\, p_3\, \psi_x \,\big]^{\mathsf{T}}, $$

i.e. a scalar field $\psi_x$ scaled by the readout time $t$ and projected onto
the PE unit vector $(p_1, p_2, p_3)$. Because the displacement is confined to
the PE direction, distortion appears as local stretching/compression (and
signal pile-up) along one image axis.

## The susceptibility field $h$ is fixed in scanner space

The **susceptibility-induced** field $h$ arises from the subject's own
tissue/air interfaces and is (to first order) fixed relative to the scanner's
$B_0$ — but it is tied to the subject's *anatomy*. This distinction is the
crux of the integrated framework: when the subject moves by a rigid transform
$r$, the field must be **rotated with the subject**, written $\omega(h, r)$
("field obtained by rigid-body transform of field $h$ with $r$"). Contrast this
with the eddy-current field $e(\beta)$, which is generated by the gradients and
so stays fixed in scanner space ([[concept-eddy-current-distortion]]).

## Correcting it

$h$ is not estimated by `eddy` itself; it is **supplied** (e.g. from a `topup`
field-map computed from reversed-PE acquisitions) and then combined with the
eddy field and the movement in one resampling step — see the forward model and
Jacobian intensity modulation in [[concept-image-registration]]
(Eqs. 2–5). Intensity is modulated by the Jacobian determinant because
compression concentrates signal and stretching dilutes it.

## Grounding in the project

nifreeze operates on data whose susceptibility distortion has typically been
handled upstream in a NiPreps pipeline (e.g. SDCFlows/`topup`); this page is the
theory of *what that correction removes* and why it must move with the subject
rather than the scanner — a constraint any reimplementation must respect.
